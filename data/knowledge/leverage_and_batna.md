# Leverage, BATNA, and competitive alternatives

Your leverage is the strength of your best alternative to a negotiated agreement
(BATNA), and your read of theirs. Improve your BATNA before the conversation and
the whole range moves.

## When the other side cites a competitor's quote
A competing quote is a leverage play, not always a fact. Probe it: is it
truly comparable scope, or apples-to-oranges? Acknowledge the alternative without
panicking, then reframe around switching costs, risk, and total value — the
things a cheaper competitor doesn't cover. Do not reflexively match the price;
match the *value gap* instead, or trade a smaller discount for commitment.

## Manufacturing and resisting urgency
Deadline pressure ("this price is only good through quarter-end") manufactures
scarcity. Test whether the deadline is real. If you don't need to close now, your
patience is leverage — name it calmly. If the other side needs to close, their
deadline is your leverage.

## Reservation point discipline
Know your walk-away before you start and never cross it to "save" a deal. The
willingness to walk is the single most reliable source of leverage.
