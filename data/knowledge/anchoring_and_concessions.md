# Anchoring and concession strategy

The first number on the table exerts a strong gravitational pull on the final
outcome. Anchoring high (as a seller) or low (as a buyer) shifts the bargaining
range in your favor, even when the other side knows it's an anchor.

## Countering an aggressive anchor
Do not counter near their anchor — that legitimizes it. Re-anchor sharply in the
opposite direction and justify your number with an external standard (market
comps, prior pricing, budget reality). Then bring both sides toward the middle
on your terms.

## Concession patterns signal information
Shrinking concessions ($10k, then $4k, then $1k) signal you're approaching your
limit and are more credible. Equal or growing concessions invite more pushing.
Never concede twice in a row without getting something back — trade every
concession for a reciprocal move (longer term, a reference, faster signature).

## The flinch and the silence
React visibly to an extreme offer; silence after a number pressures the other
side to negotiate against themselves. Most people fill silence with a concession.
